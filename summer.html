Seasons

Summer

夏 / natsu

Above the clouds.

June — August
The snow is gone but the mountains remember it. Snowmelt feeds the waterfalls. It cools the sake cellars. It fills the moat where, in July, nineteen hectares of lotus bloom on water that was ice four months ago. Higher up, above the treeline, alpine marshes hold flowers that bloom for two weeks a year. And at sea level, a port town lights five thousand fireworks over the river mouth, the way it has for five hundred years.
Summer

What the snowmelt brings.

— 01

Mt. Hiuchi & Tengu-no-niwa

火打山・天狗の庭 / Hiuchi-san, Tengu-no-niwa

The highest peak in Niigata Prefecture at 2,462 metres, and one of Japan's Hundred Famous Mountains. The trail from Sasagamine passes through Tengu-no-niwa — a high-altitude marshland that CNN listed among the world's most beautiful places. It is the northernmost habitat of the ptarmigan, Japan's protected alpine bird. The summit takes four and a half hours, and the alpine flowers peak in late July.

— 02

Takada lotus

高田の蓮 / Takada no hasu

Nineteen hectares of lotus fill the outer moat of Takada Castle — one of the largest lotus ponds on earth. It began in 1871, when a man named Hosaka Sadakichi planted lotus commercially after his domain went bankrupt. The paleobotanist Oga Ichiro later called it the most Oriental lotus he had seen. The festival runs mid-July to mid-August. The flowers open at dawn and close by noon.

— 03

Naoetsu Gion Festival

直江津祇園祭 / Naoetsu Gion Matsuri

Five hundred and thirty years of tradition, held the last week of July. Mikoshi processions through the port streets. Float parades and taiko drums. Five thousand fireworks over the mouth of the Seki River on the final night. Rice-barrel offerings on the 29th. Naoetsu has been a Sea of Japan port since the Kamakura period, and the festival has the weight of that history.

— 04

Mt. Myoko summit

妙高山 / Myōkō-san

A 2,454-metre sacred stratovolcano, climbed by pilgrims since 708 AD. The Tsubame Trail from Tsubame Onsen is the most popular route — eight to nine hours round trip through beech forest, past sulphur vents, splitting at a fork between the Kita-jigokudani Valley and the 80-metre Sotaki Falls route. From the summit: three hundred and sixty degrees of Northern Alps, Sea of Japan, and on a clear day, Sado Island.

— 05

Naoetsu Beach

直江津海岸 / Naoetsu kaigan

A wide, shallow, sandy shore on the Sea of Japan coast — rare on this side of the country. The water is warm enough for swimming from July through August. Dolphins are sometimes spotted offshore. The Buddhist monk Shinran landed here in 1207, exiled from Kyoto, and spent four years in the area. The beach at Kotagahama still marks the spot.

— 06

Gangi in summer

雁木通り / Gangi-dōri

Sixteen kilometres of covered wooden arcades — the longest system in Japan — thread through old Takada. Built in the Edo period to keep walkways passable under heavy snow, they serve a different purpose in summer: cool, shaded passages through machiya townhouses, past dye shops and coopers that have been here for centuries. The uniform width is 1.80 metres, just enough for two people and a conversation.

"A nineteen-hectare moat of lotus blooms that began as a bankrupt domain's last resort — Takada in summer is proof that necessity, left alone long enough, becomes beauty."
