Snow country, quietly.

About this place
Two hours from Tokyo by bullet train, where the Japan Sea meets a 2,400-meter volcano. A castle town with thirteen meters of annual snowfall, a thousand-year history, and food that rarely leaves the prefecture.

We write about what's actually here.
Discover

Where to begin.

— 01

Shop

買 / kau

Pottery from the kilns of Joetsu, hand-forged blades, paper, textiles. The covered arcades of Takada — gangi — are a living museum.

— 02

Eat

食 / taberu

Hand-rolled hegi soba, sake from breweries founded in the 1800s, and sea fish that still tastes of the morning catch.

— 03

See

観 / miru

One of Japan's three great night sakura at Takada Castle. Mt. Myoko, a sacred mountain since the 8th century. Powder you have to ski to believe.

— 04

Stay

泊 / tomaru

Hot-spring inns where the water has flowed for centuries, mountain lodges, and quiet farmhouses in the rice fields.

A year here

Four very different places.

冬
Winter
Dec — Mar

Thirteen to sixteen meters of snow. Steam rising from outdoor onsen. New sake pressed in the breweries. The quietest season.

春
Spring
Apr — May

Four thousand cherry trees light up around Takada Castle. Snowmelt fills the rivers. The rice paddies turn to mirrors.

夏
Summer
Jun — Aug

Mountain hiking above the clouds. Lotus blooms on the castle moat. Festival lanterns through the gangi at night.

秋
Autumn
Sep — Nov

Rice harvest. New-crop sake. Foliage from the peaks down to the sea. The clearest skies of the year.
