See

観 / miru

A sacred mountain, a castle in bloom, and thirteen metres of snow.

What's here
A castle that was built in four months by thirteen feudal armies. A volcano that pilgrims have climbed since the eighth century. A waterfall nicknamed 'Earthquake' for the sound it makes. Covered arcades stretching sixteen kilometres through a town that gets buried in snow every winter. None of it was built for tourists. All of it is worth seeing.
Featured

What to see.

— 01

Takada Castle

高田城 / Takada-jō

Built in 1614 for Tokugawa Ieyasu's sixth son. Thirteen daimyo houses contributed labour; Date Masamune oversaw. The castle has no stone walls — only earth and water. In spring, four thousand cherry trees and three thousand lanterns make it one of Japan's top three night sakura. In summer, the 19-hectare moat fills with lotus.

— 02

Mt. Myoko

妙高山 / Myōkō-san

A 2,454-metre stratovolcano sacred since 708 AD. Uesugi Kenshin prayed at Sekiyama Shrine before battle. The summit trail from Tsubame Onsen takes eight hours round trip — through beech forest, past sulphur vents, to a 360-degree view of the Northern Alps and the Sea of Japan.

— 03

Mt. Hiuchi

火打山 / Hiuchi-san

Niigata's highest peak at 2,462 metres. The trail passes Tengu-no-niwa — a high-altitude marshland that CNN listed among the world's most beautiful spots. In summer, alpine flowers bloom around the northernmost ptarmigan habitat in Japan.

— 04

Naena Falls

苗名滝 / Naena-no-taki

Fifty-five metres of water crashing onto basalt. One of Japan's 100 Famous Waterfalls, nicknamed 'Earthquake Falls' for the sound. In spring, snowmelt doubles the volume. In autumn, the cliff walls turn gold. A fifteen-minute flat walk from the car park.

— 05

Kasugayama Castle

春日山城 / Kasugayama-jō

The mountain fortress of Uesugi Kenshin — the 'Dragon of Echigo' and one of the Sengoku period's most feared warlords. No stone, no keep — just earthen ramparts on a 180-metre summit. The thirty-minute walk to the top passes the shrine where Kenshin prayed before the Battle of Kawanakajima.

— 06

Gangi Arcades

雁木通り / Gangi-dōri

Sixteen kilometres of covered walkways threading through the old castle town of Takada. Each shopkeeper built an arcade over the sidewalk so pedestrians could pass beneath the snow. Nearly 200 shops still line the Honmachi arcade. Snow Country engineering, three centuries before the word existed.

"None of it was built to be photographed. It was built to be lived in, prayed at, walked through, survived. That it happens to be beautiful is almost incidental."
— From the editors
